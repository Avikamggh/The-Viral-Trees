Pathways to Space

Comprehensive Space & STEM Education

At SHE ORBITS, we believe the future of space must be inclusive, interdisciplinary, and global. Our 30 sectors provide girls aged 13-25 with diverse pathways to become innovators, researchers, and decision-makers shaping humanity's cosmic journey.

Complete Curriculum

30 Sectors of Space Innovation

From STEM foundations to interstellar exploration, discover your path to the stars

01 Foundation

STEM

Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics - The foundational pillar developing analytical reasoning, problem-solving, and innovation skills critical for aerospace.

02 Business

Space Industry

Commercial, industrial, and entrepreneurial dimensions of the global space economy, from traditional aerospace to New Space ventures.

03 Technology

Space Navigation

Movement and positioning of spacecraft, satellites, and rovers including orbital mechanics, trajectory planning, and GNSS systems.

04 Technology

Space Communications

Science and technology of transmitting data between spacecraft, satellites, and Earth using radio, optical, and quantum systems.

05 Engineering

Space Technology

Tools, machines, and systems enabling exploration including propulsion, robotics, autonomous systems, sensors, and materials.

06 Life Sciences

Space Medicine

How space conditions affect human health, focusing on microgravity, radiation, nutrition, physiology, and mental well-being.

07 Life Sciences

Space Pharmacy

Development, preservation, and delivery of medicines in space including drug stability, biomanufacturing, and pharmaceutical systems.

08 Sustainability

Space Agriculture

Farming methods for extraterrestrial environments including hydroponics, aeroponics, and closed-loop ecological systems.

09 Design

Space Architecture & Habitat Design

Design of human habitats in extreme environments merging engineering, architecture, and human-centered sustainability.

10 Environment

Space Environment

Unique environmental challenges including radiation, microgravity, vacuum conditions, and orbital debris management.

11 Policy

Sustainability & Space Policy

Ethical, legal, and political frameworks for sustainable exploration including treaties, governance, and resource access.

12 Earth Sciences

Earth Observation & Climate Science

Satellites and space-based technologies monitoring Earth's systems including climate, land use, and natural disasters.

13 Engineering

Satellite Design & CubeSats

Design, construction, and operation of small satellites democratizing access to space research and innovation.

14 Defense

Planetary Defense & Asteroid Science

Detecting, tracking, and mitigating threats from near-Earth objects while studying asteroids as scientific resources.

15 Governance

Space Law, Policy & Ethics

Legal, regulatory, and ethical frameworks governing outer space including treaties, ownership, and equitable access.

16 Business

Space Economics & Business Innovation

Financial and entrepreneurial dimensions from launch costs to emerging markets like lunar mining and tourism.

17 Technology

AI & Data Science in Space Exploration

Artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data analytics for mission planning, discovery, and autonomous operations.

18 Communication

Space Arts & Science Communication

Art, storytelling, and media to communicate space science in engaging, human-centered ways to global audiences.

19 Engineering

Materials Science & Advanced Manufacturing

Developing materials for extreme conditions and advanced methods like 3D printing for on-site construction.

20 Exploration

Deep Space Missions & Interstellar Exploration

Missions beyond Earth orbit including travel to outer solar system, icy moons, and interstellar probes.

21 Life Sciences

Astrobiology & Life Sciences

Origins, evolution, and possibility of life in the universe combining biology, chemistry, and astronomy.

22 Science

Astrophysics & Astronomy

Physics of the universe from galaxies and stars to black holes and cosmology through observation and study.

23 Science

Planetary Science & Geology

Structure, composition, and processes of planets, moons, and asteroids combining geology, chemistry, and physics.

24 Technology

GNSS & GIS

Global Navigation Satellite Systems and Geographic Information Systems for positioning, navigation, and mapping.

25 Society

Future Human Society in Space

How human communities will live, work, and govern in off-Earth environments like lunar colonies or Martian cities.

26 Security

Cybersecurity in Space Infrastructure

Protection of satellites, spacecraft, and communication networks from cyberattacks and data breaches.

27 Human Factors

Space Psychology & Human Factors

Human behavior, mental health, and group dynamics in space missions focusing on isolation, confinement, and stress.

28 Energy

Space Energy & Power Systems

How missions and settlements generate, store, and use energy including solar, nuclear, and wireless power systems.

29 Resources

Lunar Economy & Resource Utilization

Using lunar resources (water ice, regolith, metals) for human settlement and industrial growth through ISRU.

30 Culture

Philosophy of Space & Cultural Heritage

Cultural, ethical, and philosophical questions of humanity's expansion including heritage preservation and identity.
